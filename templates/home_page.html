Earthquakes occur all over the world everyday

I experienced a massive earthquake with magnitude 9.0 in Japan, 11th March 2011.
Many people died not from the earthquake but from the tsunami caused by it. Nuclear power plants also lost control and were in danger of meltdown. Public services were shut down, electricity was in short supply, food was not available in supermarkets, and such conditions persisted for a while even in my country, which is a developed country.

And most recently, on 6th February 2023, magnitude 7.5 earthquake hit Turkey.
It caused damage to buildings and many casualties. There are 290 aftershocks of magnitude 4.0 or higher had been recorded in Turkey by 21th Febrary.

Earthquakes can happen suddenly and anywhere in the world. How often do you think earthquakes actually occur in the world today? You can find it on this website. This website displays the data of earthquakes that occurred within a 19day, 1st to 19th February 2023.
The data is from open source; USGS Earthquake Hazards Program.
